Sam Altman's "The Gentle Singularity" describes intelligence becoming an ambient utility — too cheap to meter, personalized for everyone, requiring no technical prerequisites. Not a dramatic rupture, but a gradual integration where AI becomes as unremarkable and universally available as electricity. The transition happens through steady reduction of friction, not a single breakthrough.

This maps directly onto the accessibility race of 2026. When Altman writes about intelligence "easy for everyone," he's describing the principle behind Claude Channels, OpenClaw, and every tool putting AI into messaging apps. The terminal, the API key, the developer-as-intermediary — all friction the industry is racing to eliminate. Altman provides the philosophical framework; products like Channels are the implementation.
